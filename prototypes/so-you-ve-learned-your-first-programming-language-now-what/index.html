So you've learned your first programming language... Here's the next step.

Published: 2020-07-13
Author: NostraDavid

I believe there's a knowledge gap for beginners that know the basics (i.e. a single programming language), but don't know what to do after finishing something like "Head First Java"

I'll go over the things you (IMO) should learn to become a better programmer.

It's rougly in order, but it's OK if you want to learn things differently. Always adapt someone else's ideas to fit your own situation. Never blindly adopt an idea.

Note that this is my personal idea of how things should be, could be: It's fine to disagree

What you should already know

First I want to go over the things I expect you to know. If you don't: That's OK, because now you know what you don't know and you can take that into consideration.

UML

It's one of those things you've probably seen, but you don't know a name for any of it, but if it's a diagram in a programming article, it's probably based on UML, the Unified Modeling Language

Patterns and Frameworks

So you know how a class works, but how do you expand the patterns of a single class over multiple ones?

Ever wondered how RTS' like Command and Conquer let you give a unit multiple commands for them to follow? There's a pattern for that

Have you ever wondered how an undo/redo system from Notepad worked? There's a pattern for that.

Use "Head First Patterns" to learn yourself the basics and use "Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software" as documentation/quick lookup, once you get the hang of it.

The authors of Design Patterns are referred to as the Gang of Four (GoF)

Protip: You can use use this to train yourself to learn UML

Do try to be careful: When I just learned about patterns, they were a hammer and everything else started to look like a nail. Do try not to fall into the same trap :)
